# DRHP Red Flag Checklist

The 10 red-flag categories that every DRHP analysis must score. Each is GREEN / YELLOW / RED with verbatim evidence + page citation.

---

## 1. Negative cash flows alongside positive profits

**What to check:** CFO < 0 in any of last 3 years while PAT > 0; OR cumulative 3Y CFO/PAT < 0.6.

**Why it matters:** Either earnings are inflated (channel stuffing, aggressive revenue recognition) or working-capital is deteriorating in a way that should worry investors before they buy.

**RED if:** Any year of negative CFO with positive PAT, OR 3Y CFO/PAT < 0.5.
**YELLOW if:** 3Y CFO/PAT in 0.5-0.7.
**GREEN if:** CFO/PAT > 0.7 each year.

---

## 2. Sudden profit spike before IPO

**What to check:** PAT growth in the year before filing materially exceeds 3-year CAGR; revenue growth doesn't justify margin expansion; revenue and PAT both jump in IPO year.

**Why it matters:** Window dressing is common — companies time their IPO to coincide with a peak earnings year, sometimes via cost cuts that aren't sustainable.

**RED if:** PAT growth in IPO year > 2× 3Y PAT CAGR AND margin expansion not explained by operational change.
**YELLOW if:** PAT growth > 1.5× 3Y CAGR with explanation but explanation is thin.
**GREEN if:** PAT growth in line with revenue growth and 3-year trend.

---

## 3. Large OFS component

**What to check:** OFS component as % of total issue size.

**Why it matters:** A heavy OFS means promoters / PE investors / employees are cashing out. Money does not go to the company; it goes to selling shareholders. If the issuers had high conviction in the future, they wouldn't sell so much.

**RED if:** OFS > 60% of total issue.
**YELLOW if:** OFS 30-60%.
**GREEN if:** OFS < 30%.

Note: An OFS by a PE investor with a 7-10 year exit timeline is different from an OFS by the founding promoter. Distinguish in the evidence.

---

## 4. Auditor qualifications or change

**What to check:** Auditor opinion in restated financials (qualified / emphasis of matter / adverse / disclaimer); any auditor change in last 3 years and stated reason; auditor reputation.

**Why it matters:** Per Day 1's case studies (Manpasand, Brightcom), auditor changes / resignations / qualifications precede every major Indian fraud disclosure.

**RED if:** Any qualified opinion in last 3 years; OR 2+ auditor changes; OR resignation citing non-cooperation.
**YELLOW if:** 1 auditor change with reasonable explanation; OR Emphasis of Matter on a material item.
**GREEN if:** Same reputable auditor for 3+ years; clean unqualified opinions.

---

## 5. Heavy client / supplier concentration

**What to check:** Revenue from top 3 / top 5 / top 10 customers as % of total revenue (3-year trend).

**Why it matters:** A single-customer-dependent business is fragile; the customer can dictate terms. Common in B2B / specialty manufacturers / IT services.

**RED if:** Top 1 customer > 30% of revenue; OR top 3 > 60%.
**YELLOW if:** Top 1 in 15-30%; OR top 3 in 40-60%.
**GREEN if:** Top 1 < 15% AND top 3 < 40%.

For supplier concentration, similar bands apply on input-cost side.

---

## 6. Pending legal proceedings

**What to check:** "Outstanding Litigation" annexure for cases against the company, against promoters, against KMPs.

Categories to score:
- **Tax disputes** — common, often less concerning unless quantified-large
- **Civil litigation** (commercial disputes) — moderate
- **Criminal proceedings** against promoter/KMP — RED automatically
- **SEBI / regulatory** action against promoter — RED automatically
- **Class actions** or large environmental disputes — investigate

**RED if:** Any criminal proceeding against promoter/KMP; OR any SEBI action; OR aggregate quantum of civil disputes > 10% of post-IPO net worth.
**YELLOW if:** Multiple ongoing tax disputes aggregating > 5% of net worth.
**GREEN if:** Routine / minor tax matters only.

---

## 7. Promoter compensation extreme

**What to check:** Total promoter + KMP remuneration for last 3 years as % of PAT.

**Why it matters:** Disproportionate extraction signals governance concerns even before a fraud emerges.

**RED if:** Promoter compensation > 8% of PAT in any year.
**YELLOW if:** 4-8% of PAT.
**GREEN if:** < 4% of PAT.

Adjust expectations for sector — service businesses (consulting, asset management) routinely have higher comp ratios.

---

## 8. Related Party Transactions — value extraction signature

**What to check:** Specific RPT patterns (regardless of total quantum) that suggest value extraction:

- Royalty / brand-fee paid to a promoter entity > 1% of revenue
- Property rented from promoter at above-market rates
- Loans / advances to promoter entities (especially without commercial rationale)
- RPT counter-party in same line of business as the IPO company
- Sales to / purchases from promoter entities at non-market terms

**RED if:** Any of: brand-fee > 2% of revenue, loans > 5% of net worth to promoter entities, sales > 10% to promoter-controlled entities (unless captive supplier business model).
**YELLOW if:** Brand-fee 1-2%; OR moderate property rental from promoters.
**GREEN if:** RPTs minimal and look arms-length.

---

## 9. Working capital cycle stretched in IPO year

**What to check:** DSO, inventory days, payable days for 3 years. Often companies stretch working capital in IPO year to inflate revenue (push channel) or look more profitable.

**RED if:** DSO drift > +20 days in IPO year vs 3-year prior average; OR inventory days drift > +40 days.
**YELLOW if:** Drift +10 to +20 days (DSO) or +20 to +40 days (inventory).
**GREEN if:** No material drift.

---

## 10. Industry section dependency on a single paid consultant

**What to check:** The "Industry Overview" section — is it dominated by data from a single industry-research firm (CRISIL, Frost & Sullivan, Technopak, Wazir)? Is the company a paying client?

**Why it matters:** These reports are commissioned by the issuer; market-share and TAM claims should be cross-verified against independent sources before being believed. SEBI mandates disclosure of the consultant; check.

**RED if:** The DRHP's growth narrative depends materially on one paid report's projections AND those projections cannot be independently verified.
**YELLOW if:** Single consultant cited but projections are roughly aligned with public industry data.
**GREEN if:** Multiple independent sources triangulated; or sector data is well-publicly-known.

---

## How the red-flag count drives subscription view

| Count of RED flags | Implied subscription view |
|---|---|
| 0 | SUBSCRIBE (subject to valuation check from Dimension §9) |
| 1-2 | SUBSCRIBE-FOR-LISTING-GAINS-ONLY or WATCH-POST-LISTING |
| 3+ | AVOID |

Count of YELLOW flags is informational; it modifies confidence in the SUBSCRIBE view but doesn't override it.

## Special situations

- **SME IPO:** Lower bar on disclosure depth; recalibrate thresholds slightly. SME platform RPT/promoter checks remain unchanged.
- **FPO / Follow-on:** Skip §3 OFS analysis but add: dilution impact, EPS impact post-issue, was the prior IPO's promised use-of-proceeds executed?
- **Pre-IPO placement / anchor book disclosure:** Read who's anchoring; reputable institutional anchors lend some credibility, but anchor allocations don't immunise against the red flags above.
