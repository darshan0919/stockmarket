## What this example teaches

1. **Phase sequence matters.** Running the concall before the P&L diff would have missed the 44% Q4 revenue concentration and the real meaning of the CFO/PAT drop.

2. **Stale price data is the #1 trap.** Screener.in showed ₹886 (Jan 20 close) while the live price was ₹1,166.75. The whole repricing would have been wrong if the stale source was trusted.

3. **Silent items are data points.** ASPL's absence from a post-results concall is as material as any disclosed information. Institutional analysts weight this heavily.

4. **One verbatim quote can shift the model.** The CFO's "margins will keep improving, not normalizing at 15%" is worth more than 50 pages of deck analysis. Hunt for these specifically.

5. **The rank shift matters as much as the absolute valuation.** WAAREERTL moving from #1 to #3 in the universe is the actionable insight — tells an allocator where to redeploy marginal capital (to EMMVEE or KPIGREEN).
